# Copy-ready Prompt (English)

## Full version

Remake this photo as an early Source-engine-style 3D game screenshot from around 2004.

Strictly preserve the original subject, composition, crop, viewpoint, perspective, pose, spatial layout, and key recognition anchors. Do not apply a surface filter. Break the entire photo into old-game assets and rebuild it.

Render people, architecture, trees, roads, rocks, furniture, food, and all objects with clearly simplified low-poly geometry. Reduce curves and tiny details; use hard edges and readable chunky forms. Cover all surfaces with low-resolution textures that are slightly blurry, dirty, repeated, and limited in detail. Mild stretching and visible seams are acceptable, as in inexpensive game assets from around 2004.

Simulate early-3D-engine lighting: stiff, mildly inconsistent direct light and baked shadows. Avoid cinematic global illumination, realistic reflections, ray tracing, and modern PBR materials. People should become recognizable low-poly NPC models while preserving face shape, hairstyle, glasses, clothing, pose, and key accessories. Do not turn them into different people.

The final image must look like a genuinely playable 2004-era game-map screenshot—not pixelation, blur, mosaic, or a retro filter. Avoid modern AAA game quality, anime, illustration, and cartoon aesthetics. Do not add text, people, buildings, HUD elements, borders, or watermarks, and do not remove key source-image elements.

Rebuild people, architecture, vegetation, roads, and objects as visible low-poly models with slightly blurry, dirty, repeated low-resolution textures and stiff early-game lighting. ## Intensity add-ons

### Light

Preserve more source detail and visual polish. Simplify geometry and materials moderately while keeping a clear but gentle old-game character.

### Standard

Clearly reduce polygon count and texture fidelity. Strengthen polygonal planes, low-resolution materials, and stiff lighting while keeping the subject and scene recognizable.

### Heavy

Strongly reduce polygon count and texture detail. Allow repeated, stretched, and visibly joined textures, with cheap-asset-pack and 2004 custom-map roughness, while strictly preserving subject, composition, and spatial layout.
