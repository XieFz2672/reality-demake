# Troubleshooting and Repair Patches

## Still looks like a filtered photo

> Do not preserve photographic surfaces. Rebuild the people, architecture, vegetation, roads, and objects as visible low-poly 3D models. Strengthen polygonal planes, hard silhouettes, low-resolution textures, and old-game asset character.

## Too polished or modern

> Remove PBR, advanced reflections, depth-of-field, ray tracing, and cinematic lighting. Reduce polygon count and material fidelity. Use rough low-resolution textures, stiff direct lighting, baked shadows, and cheap asset-pack quality.

## Only blurred or pixelated

> Do not create the effect through blur, mosaics, or enlarged pixels. Rebuild genuine low-poly geometry so people, architecture, and objects have visible polygonal planes.

## The person became someone else

> Strictly lock the original face shape, relative facial-feature placement, hairstyle, glasses, pose, clothing silhouette, and key accessories. Reduce only mesh and texture fidelity; do not redesign the person.

## Composition changed

> Realign to the source camera angle, horizon, perspective lines, foreground/midground/background relationships, and object positions. Do not move, enlarge, shrink, or rearrange major elements.

## Subject or key prop disappeared

> Restore the original subject and every key prop at the original position, scale, pose, and orientation. Stylization may change only geometry, materials, and lighting.

## Low-poly effect is too weak

> Further reduce curves and small details. Strengthen simplified polygonal planes on people, architecture, trees, rocks, and objects. Make edges harder and forms chunkier while keeping the subject recognizable.

## Lighting is too dark

> Keep early-game lighting stiff, but raise ambient visibility and subject readability. Avoid crushed black shadows; keep the person and scene structure clear.

## Vegetation looks too modern

> Convert trees and plants into early-game assets: simplify trunk and canopy geometry, reduce leaf detail, and use low-resolution repeated textures with limited layers. Avoid modern photoreal vegetation.

## Food does not read as a game prop

> Preserve the food category, arrangement, container, and main colors. Rebuild it as a collectible old-game prop or environment asset with a low-poly silhouette, low-resolution materials, and slightly stiff highlights. Do not add inventory UI.
