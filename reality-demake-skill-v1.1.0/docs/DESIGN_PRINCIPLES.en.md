# Design Principles

## 1. Structural demake before image degradation

A convincing demake comes from rebuilding geometry, textures, and lighting. Lower resolution, enlarged pixels, noise, and compression artifacts are secondary tools only.

## 2. Recognition before roughness

The image may be rough, but the subject must remain present, people must remain recognizable, and the scene must not be arbitrarily rearranged.

## 3. Low-poly is not cartoon

Avoid rounded, cute, toy-like, or modern stylized rendering. The target is hard edges, limited textures, and awkward lighting associated with early PC 3D games.

## 4. Navigable spatial logic

Preserve paths, entrances, stairs, corridors, streams, and architectural relationships so the output feels like a playable level rather than a flat poster.

## 5. Reference an era, not proprietary assets

Use the rendering characteristics of the mid-2000s as reference, but do not reproduce specific characters, maps, logos, or proprietary assets.
